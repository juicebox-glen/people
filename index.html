/COLLABORATION

We work closely with our clients from the outset, building ideas through conversation rather than fixed briefs.

The process is open, iterative, and shaped together.

1

Discovery

Every project begins by understanding what is already there. The building, the context, the light, and how the space is used day to day.

2

Making

Ideas are tested through drawing, models, and material studies. This is where concepts are refined into something real, considered, and buildable.

3

Construction

We carry projects through to completion, working closely with contractors and specialists. Ensuring what is built reflects the thinking behind it.

Type

Research & product

Location

Stockholm region · Nordic test courts

Status

Field lab, ongoing iteration

Scope

Full-scale wear tests, player feedback, supplier co-development, segment-replaceable system design

Materials

Cork Studio draws the room toward warmth and tactility—linseed underfoot, soft light on cork, and a quiet rhythm between bar and booth.

The narrative folds together the plainspoken lines of mid-century interiors and the ease of a late night as the room thins out and the city keeps moving outside.

Cork Studio draws the room toward warmth and tactility—linseed underfoot, soft light on cork.

Recycled content in the surface layer is balanced with grip and UV stability so outdoor courts don't trade one problem for another.

Documentation is written for facilities managers—repeatable maintenance without a brand team on site.

Cork & recycled rubber

Wall and bar cladding use cork for warmth and acoustics; the surface layer blends recycled rubber with UV-stable binders.

Linseed-finished timber

Underfoot, linseed oil–finished timber ties the room to a maintainable, honest material palette.

UV-stable surface system

The outdoor segment system is judged on accelerated UV cycles and real club sessions alike.
