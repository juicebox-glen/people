Our beliefs

At PEOPLE, architecture is approached as a responsibility. Decisions made today shape how spaces are used and understood over time. Every project begins by understanding what is already there. The building, the context, the light, and how people already use the space.

Ideas are developed through conversation, drawing, and making. Clients are part of the process, helping shape outcomes that respond to real life rather than assumption. We design for use, routine, and change. Not just how a space looks, but how it feels to live in, and how it endures.

Our process

Every project begins with people. How a space is actually used, day to day, over time.

We build ideas through conversation rather than fixed briefs. Tested through drawing and making, and carried through to construction.

PROJECT

Balmore House

Type

Research & product

Location

Stockholm region · Nordic test courts

Status

Field lab, ongoing iteration

Scope

Full-scale wear tests, player feedback, supplier co-development, segment-replaceable system design

Materials

This end of terrace home on the edge of Highgate and Hampstead Heath is an exercise in how to deal with an oddly shaped urban plot.

Being adjacent to a footpath with a triangular footprint, the site needed a response which balanced privacy, security, daylight and all the other needs of modern life. The property was extended at ground level to the rear and side as well as a full house refurbishment. We were involved throughout the process including a full interior design package to ensure continuity.

Balmore House draws the room toward warmth and tactility—linseed underfoot, soft light on cork.

Recycled content in the surface layer is balanced with grip and UV stability so outdoor courts don't trade one problem for another.

Documentation is written for facilities managers—repeatable maintenance without a brand team on site.

Cork & recycled rubber

Wall and bar cladding use cork for warmth and acoustics; the surface layer blends recycled rubber with UV-stable binders.

Linseed-finished timber

Underfoot, linseed oil–finished timber ties the room to a maintainable, honest material palette.

UV-stable surface system

The outdoor segment system is judged on accelerated UV cycles and real club sessions alike.

Checkers Barn

Type

Sport infrastructure

Location

Oslo region · coastal pilot

Status

Pilot complete, scale-up review

Scope

Outdoor court surfacing, drainage, perimeter lighting, winterisation tests

Materials

Checkers Barn tests full-scale outdoor play under Nordic weather—freeze, thaw, and glare—before a surface ships to clubs.

The work pairs instrumented slabs with player sessions so performance data and subjective feel stay in the same conversation.

Drainage, grip, and UV stability are validated together—no lab result ships without a week of evening play on the slab.

Segmented surfacing lets high-wear zones be swapped without closing the whole court.

Documentation targets facility operators: replaceable parts, torque specs, and a single warranty path.

Recycled rubber wear layer

Grip and UV resistance are tuned for long summer days and salt-air exposure.

Perimeter drainage

Channels move meltwater off the playing slab without ponding at the fence line.

LED handrail detail

Low-profile lighting keeps late sessions playable without spill into neighbouring plots.

/ Materials

Commissioned March 2022
Completed June 2023
Pilot duration 14 months
Court count 2 full-size
In use since July 2023
